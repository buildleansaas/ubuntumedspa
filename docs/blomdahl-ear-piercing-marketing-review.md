## Marketing review

### Parent-first clarity

Pass. The comparison page leads with the actual decision parents are making: retail convenience vs a controlled appointment. The hub now has a “Choose your ear piercing path” section instead of forcing every visitor through one generic page.

### Competitive positioning

Pass. The “vs” page compares systems and settings, not a named competitor attack. Mall piercing is positioned as variable and convenient, while Blomdahl is positioned as controlled, appointment-based, hypoallergenic, and pediatric-aware.

### Conversion path

Pass. Each content page returns users to a concrete booking path with procedure-specific query params. Hub and support pages link back into the consult flow, the relevant intent page, and the Williamsburg/local support page.

### Trust proof

Partial pass. Provider trust is clear: Jenny Coleman, MSN, RN, CPNP, PMHS. Blomdahl trust is clear: sterile single-use cassettes, Medical Plastic, Medical Grade Titanium, hypoallergenic starter jewelry. Next proof layer should add real clinic/product/provider photos and review prompts once available.

### Local relevance

Pass. Local pages are not duplicate doorway pages. They include route/logistics, area context, local planning, nearby neighborhoods, and cross-links back to the main hub and comparison guides.

### SEO intent coverage

Pass. The content now targets:

- `Blomdahl ear piercing Williamsburg VA`

- `hypoallergenic ear piercing Williamsburg VA`

### Non-alarmist medical language

Pass. Copy avoids scare tactics and overclaims. It explains sterile equipment, starter jewelry, placement review, and aftercare in direct parent language without implying retail piercing is automatically unsafe.
