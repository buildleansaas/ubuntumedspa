# Sports Massage + PRP Outreach Playbook

## Goal

Build a small relationship pipeline with sports massage and recovery-focused bodywork providers around Williamsburg. The outreach should be educational and reciprocal, not referral-fee driven.

## Best First Targets

Prioritize providers whose public positioning overlaps with active adults, sports massage, deep tissue, trigger point work, myofascial release, or recovery care:

- Williamsburg Therapeutic Massage
- Massage & Movement / Michele Rose
- The WISC Massage Therapy
- Innovative Pain Release Center
- Bodhi Tree Wellness Center

Avoid positioning this as replacing PT. Use sports massage and recovery language first.

## Outreach Positioning

Williamsburg Med Spa can be a consultative option when a client has recurring knee, shoulder, tendon, or overuse concerns that are not resolving with bodywork alone. Jenny can help the person understand whether PRP is worth discussing and when a different medical, orthopedic, or rehab path is more appropriate.

Keep claims conservative:

- PRP is a regenerative therapy discussion, not a guaranteed fix.
- PRP does not replace urgent medical evaluation, imaging, PT, surgery, or massage when those are the right fit.
- Bodywork can remain valuable for recovery, mobility, and ongoing support.

## Email Script

Subject: Local recovery referral connection in Williamsburg

Hi [Name],

I’m reaching out from Williamsburg Med Spa. Jenny Coleman, MSN, RN, CPNP, PMHS works with patients who are exploring regenerative options like PRP, including active adults dealing with recurring knee, shoulder, tendon, or overuse concerns.

We are not trying to replace massage or bodywork. A lot of people need exactly what you provide: mobility support, recovery work, and hands-on care. We are looking to build a small local network so that when someone is not progressing with one approach alone, they have a thoughtful next place to ask questions.

If it is useful, we would be happy to introduce ourselves, learn more about the kinds of clients you see, and talk through when PRP education may or may not be appropriate.

Best,
[Name]
Williamsburg Med Spa

## Phone Script

Hi, this is [Name] with Williamsburg Med Spa. We are putting together a small local recovery network with sports massage and bodywork providers. Jenny Coleman offers PRP consultations, and we often see overlap with active adults who have recurring knee, shoulder, tendon, or overuse concerns. We are not calling about referral fees or replacing bodywork. We would like to learn when your clients may need medical education and when our patients may be better served by massage or recovery work. Who would be the best person to talk with?

## Follow-Up Offer

Offer a simple one-page PDF or email summary:

- What PRP is.
- What PRP is not.
- When a client might ask a medical provider about PRP.
- When massage/bodywork, rehab, imaging, or orthopedic care should come first.
- How to reach Williamsburg Med Spa for a consult.

## Tracking

Track outreach in a simple sheet:

- Business
- Contact name
- Website
- Phone/email
- Specialty
- Date contacted
- Response
- Follow-up date
- Notes

## Success Criteria

Within 30 days:

- Contact 5 providers.
- Have 2 real conversations.
- Get 1 warm referral path or reciprocal education conversation.
- Identify the language providers use for recurring pain, overuse, recovery, and active adults.
