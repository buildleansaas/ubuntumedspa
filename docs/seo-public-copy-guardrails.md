## Public-copy rules

- Write for a local patient deciding whether to book, not for an internal SEO review.
- Say Williamsburg, VA naturally where it helps searchers confirm local fit.
- Lead with service fit, provider trust, consultation clarity, price/next step when available, and realistic expectations.
- Keep claims conservative: no guaranteed outcomes, no surgical-equivalent claims, no fake proof, no invented testimonials, no fake before/after language.
- For PRP, O-Shot, breast lift, hair restoration, Botox, Xeomin, and fillers, state that candidacy, risks, and expected response are reviewed during consultation.
- Avoid em dashes in public-facing copy. Use commas, periods, colons, parentheses, or plain sentence breaks.
- Do not target laser hair removal or Hydrafacial until the client confirms they are actual desired offers.
- Do not create broad surrounding-city pages without real local proof and a clear conversion reason.

## Asset guidance

- Prefer real clinic, provider, product, procedure-room, consultation, and tasteful lifestyle photos where available.
- If generated placeholder assets are needed, keep them non-clinical, local, warm, and non-deceptive.
- Do not generate medical before/after images or imply treatment outcomes.
