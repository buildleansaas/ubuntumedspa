# Ear Re-Piercing in Williamsburg, VA with Blomdahl

Closed or partially closed ear piercings are common. The next step is not to force jewelry through the old hole. It is to have the area reviewed before deciding where and whether to pierce again.

Williamsburg Med Spa offers [ear re-piercing](/procedures/blomdahl-ear-piercing/for/re-piercing) with Blomdahl medical ear piercing for adults, teens, and children who need a calmer appointment-based option.

## Short answer

If a piercing is closed, crooked, irritated, or has a history of problems, book a review before trying to reopen it yourself. Jenny can evaluate placement, sensitivity history, and whether the same spot or a nearby placement makes more sense.

## Why placement review matters

Old holes, scar tissue, uneven placement, irritation, or a history of keloids can affect the plan. Sometimes the best spot is not the exact old location.

A placement review helps answer:

- Is the old channel actually open?
- Is there scar tissue that changes the placement?
- Is the old hole even enough to reuse?
- Is there active irritation that should calm down first?
- Would a nearby fresh placement look better?
- Does sensitive-ear history change starter jewelry choice?

Blomdahl uses sterile disposable piercing cassettes and hypoallergenic starter jewelry options. For re-piercing, that can support a clean, structured appointment with clear aftercare.

It also gives patients a chance to talk through starter jewelry. ## Do not force jewelry through

If the hole has narrowed or closed, forcing jewelry through can irritate tissue or create a rough channel. It can also make it harder to tell whether the piercing is truly open or just being pushed through tender skin.

Schedule a review instead.

## Re-piercing after irritation or infection

Wait until the ear is calm before re-piercing. That usually means no active tenderness, drainage, swelling, warmth, or worsening redness. If infection was suspected, get medical guidance before scheduling.

A careful re-piercing plan is especially useful if your first piercing was done in a rushed setting or if you are now comparing [Blomdahl vs mall ear piercing](/blog/medical-ear-piercing-vs-mall-piercing).
