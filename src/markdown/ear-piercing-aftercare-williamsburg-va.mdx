# Ear Piercing Aftercare After a Blomdahl Piercing

Good aftercare protects the result of a clean piercing visit. The main idea is simple: keep the area clean, avoid unnecessary touching, and do not change starter earrings too early.

Williamsburg Med Spa reviews aftercare during each [Blomdahl ear piercing appointment](/procedures/blomdahl-ear-piercing), especially for children, sensitive ears, and re-piercing patients.

## The first day

Before leaving the clinic, make sure you know:

- how to clean the piercing,
- what not to touch,
- how long starter earrings should stay in,
- what symptoms are normal early on,
- what symptoms should prompt a call,
- whether sports, swimming, or an upcoming event changes the plan.

For a child's first piercing, it helps to repeat the aftercare instructions in simple language before the ride home.

## Keep starter earrings in place

Blomdahl starter earrings should stay in through the early healing window unless Jenny gives different instructions. For earlobes, families commonly plan around several weeks of starter-earring wear before switching.

Do not change earrings early just because the ear looks calm. The inside of the piercing can still be healing.

## Keep hands away

Touching new earrings with unwashed hands can irritate the area. Children may need reminders not to twist, pull, or play with new earrings.

A simple parent rule works well: clean only the way Jenny instructed, then leave the earrings alone.

## Plan around sports, swimming, and busy weekends

If a child has sports, swimming, travel, sleepovers, or a major event coming up, mention that before scheduling. Timing matters when you want aftercare to be manageable.

The best piercing appointment is not squeezed between a rushed errand and a pool party. Choose a calm window.

## Watch for concerns

Call the clinic or seek medical care if you notice:

- redness that keeps spreading,
- swelling that gets worse,
- warmth around the piercing,
- drainage,
- fever,
- increasing pain,
- an earring that feels embedded or too tight.

Parents do not need to panic over every normal healing change. The goal is to know what to watch for and ask early when something looks wrong.
