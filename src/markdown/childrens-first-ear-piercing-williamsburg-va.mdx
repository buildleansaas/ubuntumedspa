# Children's First Ear Piercing in Williamsburg, VA

A child's first ear piercing is a small appointment, but it can feel like a big decision for parents. The goal is not just to get earrings placed. The goal is a calm visit, sterile technique, appropriate starter jewelry, and clear aftercare once you leave.

Williamsburg Med Spa offers [Blomdahl Ear Piercing](/procedures/blomdahl-ear-piercing) for children and adults at the Williamsburg clinic. Parents can also review the dedicated [children's ear piercing page](/procedures/blomdahl-ear-piercing/for/children).

## When is a child ready for ear piercing?

Readiness depends on age, parent preference, health history, and whether the child can tolerate a brief appointment. Some families prefer piercing earlier, while others wait until the child can participate in the decision.

Before scheduling, think through:

- whether your child can keep hands away from new earrings,
- whether school, sports, swimming, or travel will affect aftercare,
- whether there is a history of metal sensitivity,
- whether you have time to help with cleaning and monitoring.

If your child has a medical condition, active skin issue, or allergy concern, mention it before the visit.

## Why jewelry material matters

Starter jewelry stays in during the early healing window, so material matters. Blomdahl uses hypoallergenic medical plastic and titanium options, which can be helpful for families worried about nickel sensitivity.

Parents often choose a medical-grade system because they want a more controlled process than a walk-in retail setting.

## What happens during the appointment?

The visit usually includes placement discussion, cleaning, piercing, and aftercare review. Parents should expect time to ask questions before the piercing happens.

The appointment is designed to be organized and calm. That matters most for children who are nervous, sensitive to busy settings, or getting earrings for the first time.

## Aftercare basics

Aftercare instructions should come from the provider at the visit. In general, parents should keep the area clean, avoid unnecessary touching, and watch for unusual redness, swelling, drainage, pain, or fever.

Call the clinic or seek medical advice if something does not seem right.
